LONDON -The Swinging City

London is the capital of the United Kingdom (UK), and its largest city. It is also the city with the highest population in the UK. The population is just under 9 million. The city is the largest in western Europe by population and area. London's original city centre, the City of London is England's smallest city. In 2011 it had 7,375 inhabitants on an area of 1.12 square miles. The term "London" is used for the urban region which developed around this city centre. This area forms the region of London, the Greater London administrative unit led by the Mayor of London and the London Assembly. The City of Westminster, to the west of the City of London, has for centuries hosted the national government and parliament. Since the 19th century, the name "London" also refers to the metropolis around this core, historically split among the counties of Middlesex, Essex, Surrey, Kent, and Hertfordshire,which since 1965 has largely comprised Greater London, which is governed by 33 local authorities and the Greater London Authority.

Itinerary of Our London Package

Day 1: Explore Central London
Morning: Begin your day at Buckingham Palace, the official residence of the British monarch. Witness the iconic Changing of the Guard ceremony, a traditional display of British pageantry.
Late Morning: Take a leisurely stroll through St. James's Park, a picturesque green space, towards Westminster Abbey and Big Ben. While Big Ben is currently undergoing restoration works, it remains an iconic symbol of London.
Afternoon: Cross over Westminster Bridge to reach the Houses of Parliament (Palace of Westminster). Take a guided tour of Westminster Abbey, a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its stunning Gothic architecture and rich history.
Evening: Explore Covent Garden, a lively district filled with shops, restaurants, cafes, and street performers. Enjoy dinner at a charming eatery or watch a captivating street performance.
Day 2: Museums and Culture
Morning: Begin your day at the British Museum, one of the world's most renowned museums, housing a vast collection of artifacts and treasures spanning thousands of years and cultures.
Late Morning: After the museum visit, take a stroll down Oxford Street, London's famous shopping destination, or relax at Regent's Park, known for its beautiful gardens and open spaces.
Afternoon: Head to South Kensington, known as London's cultural hub, and visit the trio of iconic museums: the Natural History Museum, the Science Museum, and the Victoria and Albert Museum (V&A), each offering unique insights into art, science, and history.
